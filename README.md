# Bridging the Intention–Implementation Divide in Generative AI Adoption

### Overview

This repository contains the research design, survey instrument, analytical framework, and reproducible statistical workflow for the study:

**“Bridging the Intention–Implementation Divide in Generative AI Adoption: An Integrated Model of Psychological, Behavioral, and Social Drivers.”**

The study investigates why professionals who express strong intentions to adopt Generative AI (GAI) often fail to implement it consistently in real-world professional settings.

Using an integrated theoretical framework combining the:

- Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)
- Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)
- Big Five Personality Traits

the research explains how psychological, behavioral, contextual, and social factors influence the gap between **behavioral intention** and **actual Generative AI use.**

### Research Objectives

The study seeks to:

1. Examine how Big Five personality traits influence:
- Perceived Usefulness (PU)
- Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU)
- Perceived Behavioral Control (PBC)
2. Evaluate whether Subjective Norms (SN) moderate the relationship between personality traits and attitudes toward GAI.
3. Determine how user perceptions predict the:
- Intention–Implementation Gap (IIG)
4. Assess the effect of attitudes on behavioral intention toward GAI adoption.
5. Test whether personality traits moderate the relationship between:
- Behavioral Intention (BI)
- Actual Use Behavior (AB)

### Theoretical Framework

The model integrates constructs from TAM, TPB, and personality psychology.

#### Core Constructs
#### Personality Traits (OCEAN)
- Openness
- Conscientiousness
- Extraversion
- Agreeableness
- Neuroticism
#### Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)
- Perceived Usefulness (PU)
- Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU)
#### Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)
- Attitude Toward GAI (ATT)
- Subjective Norms (SN)
- Perceived Behavioral Control (PBC)
#### Behavioral Outcomes
- Behavioral Intention (BI)
- Actual Use Behavior (AB)
#### Derived Metric
- **Intention–Implementation Gap Score (IIGS)**
`IIGS = z(BI) – z(AB)`
A higher IIGS Score indicates stronger adoption intention relative to actual implementation.

### Research Questions & Hypotheses

#### RQ1

To what extent do Big Five personality traits predict perceptions of Generative AI?

#### Hypotheses
- H1a: Openness positively predicts PU, PEOU, and PBC.
- H1b: Conscientiousness positively predicts PU, PEOU, and PBC.
- H1c: Extraversion positively predicts PU, PEOU, and PBC.
- H1d: Agreeableness positively predicts PU, PEOU, and PBC.
- H1e: Neuroticism negatively predicts PU, PEOU, and PBC.

#### RQ2

Do subjective norms moderate the relationship between personality traits and attitudes toward GAI?

#### Hypothesis
- H2: Subjective norms significantly moderate the relationship between personality traits and attitudes.

#### RQ3

To what extent do perceptions predict the intention–implementation gap?

#### Hypotheses
- H3a: Perceived usefulness significantly predicts IIG.
- H3b: Perceived ease of use significantly predicts IIG.
- H3c: Perceived behavioral control significantly predicts IIG.

#### RQ4

How do user attitudes affect behavioral intention?

#### Hypothesis
- H4: Positive attitudes significantly predict stronger behavioral intention.

#### RQ5

Do personality traits moderate the relationship between intention and actual GAI use?

#### Hypotheses
- H5a: Openness positively moderates BI → AB.
- H5b: Conscientiousness positively moderates BI → AB.
- H5c: Extraversion positively moderates BI → AB.
- H5d: Agreeableness positively moderates BI → AB.
- H5e: Neuroticism negatively moderates BI → AB.
  
### Methodology
#### Research Design
- Quantitative cross-sectional study
- Survey-based data collection
- Professional and organizational respondents
  
#### Data Collection

The survey measures:

- Technology perceptions
- Behavioral intentions
- Actual GAI adoption
- Social influences
- Ethical/privacy concerns
- Contextual constraints
- Personality traits
  
#### Measurement Scale

Most constructs use:

**7-point Likert scale**
- 1 = Strongly Disagree
- 7 = Strongly Agree
